Black Box Investigation 💻

Intro

During this marking period in Media & Technology class Mr. Sinusas challenged us to create a model of what we think is in the Mystery Black Box. The Mystery Black Box is a box with a funnel at the top and a tube at the bottom that does unexpected things when we pour water. In class we discussed with Mr. Sinuses different perspectives on what we think is in the black box. We also collected data, and we experimented using water and graduated cylinders.

Here is an image of the black box:

Paragraph 1

For the experiment we poured different amounts of water into the graduated cylinder then we poured the water into the funnel and watched carefully to determine if the amount of water that was poured into the funnel came out really slow or really fast. In our trials we noticed a pattern of more water coming out than we just put in. An example of this is in trial 50 the Input( amount of water being poured in) was 1000ml of water and the output( the amount of water that came out) was 1907ml of water. This would help us in the future with designing a model of what we think is in the black box.

Evidence

After we were done experimenting and collecting data we then moved on two drawing our own version of the black box. When we were done with our drawings we presented them to our group mates because we needed to decide which model we would use to start building. After looking over each other's models we came up with our decision, we were going to go with Kayser's model. We decided to go with Kaycer's model because it was well developed. We then went through the process of building it.

Materials

Materials that were used in the process of building our version of the black box were a cardboard box, Hot glue gun, a funnel, Tubes and Plastic Containers, and Duct tape.

How The Black Box Was Made

  1. we gathered all of our materials together.
  2. we cut a hole at the top where the funnel went.
  3. we took our funnel and hot glued a tube to the funnel that would prevent the water from being poured into the funnel to stay in one place.
  4. we connected the tube to a cup.
  5. we then connected two more tubes on each side of the cup.
  6. after we were done connecting the tubes to each side of the cup we hot glued two big containers on each side of the card board box.
  7. we connected the tubes to the two big containers.
  8. FINALLY, we attached the tube from each container to the tube at the bottom of the box.

Paragraph 2

We tested our model and found out we needed to fix some of the problems that were noticed during the trial. There was water leaking from the bottom so we added more duct tape to the tubes to prevent leakage from the water.
